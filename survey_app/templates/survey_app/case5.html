Cherry Creek School District: Integrated Mental Health Framework

How Colorado's third-largest school district built a comprehensive, data-driven approach to student mental health and wellness

Cherry Creek Schools: A District-Wide Vision for Mental Health

With more than 54,000 students across 67 schools, Cherry Creek School District (CCSD) in Colorado faced significant challenges in addressing the growing mental health needs of its diverse student population. In response, the district developed an innovative, technology-enhanced mental health framework that has become a model for large districts nationwide.

Cherry Creek School District serves over 54,000 students across suburban Denver.

The Challenge

Before implementing their integrated framework, Cherry Creek faced several significant barriers to effectively supporting student mental health:

  • Increasing mental health needs among students, with nearly 30% reporting significant anxiety, depression, or trauma responses
  • Limited capacity with counselor-to-student ratios exceeding 350:1
  • Disparities in access to mental health resources across schools
  • Difficulty coordinating care between school-based and community providers
  • Lack of data integration across various student support systems
  • Growing concerns about student suicide risk and crisis situations

"We were seeing more students in crisis each year, but our systems weren't designed to identify struggling students early or coordinate care effectively. We needed a comprehensive approach that leveraged technology while maintaining the human connections that are essential to mental health support."

â€” Dr. Robyn Kellogg, Director of Student Wellness, Cherry Creek School District

The Solution: A Multi-Tiered Approach

In 2020, Cherry Creek launched its Integrated Mental Health Framework (IMHF), a comprehensive system designed to address the full spectrum of student mental health needs. The framework combines evidence-based practices with innovative technology solutions to create a coordinated approach across three tiers of support.

1
Universal Screening and Prevention

All students participate in universal screening through a secure digital platform three times per year, identifying early warning signs of mental health concerns. The district also implemented a comprehensive social-emotional learning curriculum supported by digital resources.

2
Early Intervention

Students identified through screening or staff referrals receive targeted support through small groups or individual check-ins. Cherry Creek developed a digital referral and tracking system that allows for seamless coordination between teachers, counselors, and mental health professionals.

3
Intensive Support

For students requiring more intensive intervention, Cherry Creek established partnerships with community mental health providers and implemented a telehealth platform to expand access to licensed therapists. A secure care coordination system ensures smooth transitions between school and community resources.

Technology Integration

Central to Cherry Creek's approach is a suite of integrated technology solutions:

  • Unified Data Platform: A secure system that integrates data from multiple sources (attendance, academics, behavior, screening results) to identify students who may need support
  • Digital Screening Tools: Evidence-based assessment instruments adapted for digital administration, with built-in protocols for follow-up
  • Care Coordination System: A HIPAA-compliant platform for managing referrals, documenting interventions, and tracking outcomes across school and community providers
  • Telehealth Infrastructure: Secure video conferencing and digital resource library for remote counseling and therapy sessions
  • Parent/Guardian Portal: A user-friendly interface allowing families to access resources, track their child's progress, and communicate with support team members
  • Crisis Response System: Digital protocols and communication tools for managing mental health emergencies

Cherry Creek's care coordination dashboard integrates student data while maintaining privacy and security.

Implementation Strategy

Cherry Creek adopted a phased implementation approach to ensure successful adoption:

  1. Planning Phase (6 months): Needs assessment, stakeholder engagement, and technology evaluation
  2. Pilot Phase (3 months): Testing systems in 10 schools with ongoing feedback and refinement
  3. Staged Rollout (1 year): Progressive implementation across all schools with continuous training and support
  4. Full Implementation (ongoing): System-wide adoption with regular evaluation and improvement

The district established a dedicated implementation team including mental health professionals, technology specialists, and school leaders who worked collaboratively throughout the process.

"What made our implementation successful was bringing together mental health expertise with technology know-how. We weren't just layering tech on top of existing practicesâ€”we were fundamentally rethinking our approach to create a truly integrated system."

Beyond these statistics, Cherry Creek has seen numerous qualitative improvements:

  • Earlier identification: Students receiving support at the first signs of distress rather than after problems escalate
  • Improved coordination: Seamless transitions between tiers of support and between school and community resources
  • Data-informed decisions: Better allocation of resources based on documented needs and outcomes
  • Reduced stigma: Normalizing mental health support through universal approaches
  • Increased equity: More consistent access to support services across all schools and demographic groups
  • Enhanced family engagement: Greater involvement of parents/guardians in supporting student mental health

While technology facilitates the process, human connections remain at the heart of Cherry Creek's approach.

Challenges and Lessons Learned

The implementation journey wasn't without obstacles. Key challenges and lessons included:

  • Data privacy concerns: Cherry Creek invested significant resources in ensuring FERPA and HIPAA compliance while still enabling effective data sharing
  • Technology adoption: Some staff initially struggled with new systems, requiring additional training and support
  • Cultural responsiveness: Early versions of screening tools needed modification to better account for cultural differences in how mental health symptoms present
  • Maintaining human connection: The district found it necessary to emphasize that technology should enhance, not replace, personal relationships
  • Sustainable funding: Developing a diversified funding approach combining district resources, grants, and community partnerships

"One of our biggest learnings was that technology is only as good as the people using it. We had to invest heavily in professional development and create a culture where mental health support was seen as everyone's responsibility, not just the counselors' job."

â€” Michelle Torres, School Psychologist, Cherry Creek High School

Future Directions

Building on their success, Cherry Creek continues to enhance their framework:

  • Expanding predictive analytics capabilities to identify at-risk students even earlier
  • Developing more targeted interventions for specific mental health needs
  • Enhancing culturally responsive practices across all tiers of support
  • Creating student-driven components that empower youth as mental health advocates
  • Expanding community partnerships to address social determinants of mental health

Conclusion

Cherry Creek School District's Integrated Mental Health Framework demonstrates how large districts can effectively combine technology with best practices in mental health support. By taking a comprehensive, data-driven approach, Cherry Creek has created a system that not only responds to crises but proactively supports student wellness across the entire district.
