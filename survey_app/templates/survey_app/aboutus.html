Our Story

K-12 Mental Health Technology was founded in 2019 by a team of educators, mental health professionals, and technology experts who recognized the growing mental health crisis affecting students across the country. We saw firsthand how schools were struggling to meet the increasing demand for mental health support with limited resources and outdated systems.


Our organization emerged from a collaborative project between school districts, university researchers, and technology innovators, with the shared mission of leveraging technology to make mental health support more accessible, effective, and integrated into the educational environment.


Today, we partner with schools nationwide to implement comprehensive digital mental health solutions that help identify students in need, connect them with appropriate resources, and monitor progress over time.

Our Mission

To transform how K-12 schools address student mental health by providing innovative, evidence-based technology solutions that increase access to support, improve outcomes, and empower school communities to prioritize wellbeing alongside academic achievement.

Our Vision

We envision a future where every school has the technological tools and resources needed to support the mental health of all students, creating educational environments where children feel safe, valued, and equipped to thrive both academically and emotionally.

Our Values

  • Accessibility: Making mental health support available to all students regardless of background or circumstances
  • Innovation: Continuously improving our technology to meet emerging needs and incorporate latest research
  • Privacy: Maintaining the highest standards of data protection and confidentiality
  • Collaboration: Working closely with schools, mental health professionals, and communities
  • Evidence-Based: Ensuring all our approaches are grounded in research and proven practices

Our Collaborators:

Headstream is grateful for the collaboration of The Jed Foundation and the Meadows Mental Health Policy Institute, who brought their deep expertise and diverse perspectives to building this resource.

Headstream convened a diverse working group of 12 leaders in the space whose collaboration resulted in a Roadmap with 10 key recommendations that can be put into action via the Navigator.

The Jed Foundation (JED) is a leading nonprofit that protects mental health and prevents suicide for teens and young adults by partnering with high schools, colleges, and districts to strengthen their mental health, substance misuse, and suicide prevention programs and systems; equipping teens and young adults with the skills and knowledge to help themselves and each other; and encouraging community awareness, understanding, and action for young adult mental health. JED is proud to be a part of this initiative because it allows the organization to better tailor its guidance for schools as they work to create holistic mental health and wellness experiences for their students.

Independent and nonpartisan, the Meadows Mental Health Policy Institute works at the intersection of policy and programs to create equitable systemic changes so all people in Texas, the United States, and the world can obtain the health care they need. Meadows is proud to be a part of this initiative to provide practical support to school districts and schools in utilizing digital mental health and well-being products, leveraging innovative solutions such as quality digital tools in one way to offset workforce needs within schools and increase access to mental health care for youth.
