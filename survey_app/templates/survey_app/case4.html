How one Texas school district implemented a comprehensive approach to student mental health and wellness

Judson Independent School District: A Vision for Student Wellness

Serving over 23,000 students across 30 campuses in the greater San Antonio area, Judson Independent School District (JISD) faced growing mental health challenges among its diverse student population. With determination and innovative thinking, district leaders transformed their approach to student wellness through strategic technology integration and community partnerships.

Judson ISD serves a diverse community of students across the San Antonio metropolitan area.

The Challenge

Before implementing their new approach, Judson ISD faced several significant challenges:

  • Limited counseling resources spread thin across 30 campus locations
  • Increasing rates of student anxiety, depression, and crisis situations
  • Inconsistent access to mental health services across the district
  • Difficulty identifying at-risk students before crisis situations developed
  • Need for better coordination between school-based and community mental health resources

"We were seeing more students in crisis each year, but our counselors were overwhelmed with academic duties. We needed a systematic approach that would allow us to identify struggling students earlier and connect them with appropriate resources quickly."

â€” Dr. Tanya Hernandez, Director of Student Services, Judson ISD

Comprehensive Solution

In 2021, Judson ISD launched a multi-tiered system of support focused on student mental health and wellness. Their approach included:

1. Technology Infrastructure

  • Early warning system: Implementation of data analytics software to identify students showing potential signs of mental health concerns based on attendance, academic performance, discipline records, and other indicators
  • Digital referral platform: Web-based system allowing teachers, staff, parents, and students to safely submit concerns about student well-being
  • Resource management dashboard: Central system tracking available mental health services and current capacity across the district

Judson's digital referral system allows for secure, efficient reporting of student wellness concerns.

2. Staff Development

  • District-wide training in Mental Health First Aid for all teachers and staff
  • Advanced trauma-informed practice training for counselors and social workers
  • Technology implementation training to ensure proper use of new digital tools
  • Regular professional learning communities focused on student mental health best practices

3. Community Partnerships

  • Telehealth partnerships providing remote access to licensed mental health professionals
  • Coordination with local universities to place counseling interns throughout the district
  • Collaboration with community mental health providers for seamless referrals
  • Family engagement programs to support student wellness at home

"The partnerships we've developed have expanded our capacity exponentially. By bringing in telehealth services and university interns, we've been able to provide more individual counseling while freeing our school counselors to focus on preventive work and crisis response."

â€” Miguel Santos, Lead School Counselor, Judson High School

Implementation Process

Judson's approach followed a carefully structured implementation timeline:

  1. Phase 1 (3 months): Needs assessment and technology evaluation
  2. Phase 2 (2 months): Staff training and technology implementation
  3. Phase 3 (1 month): Pilot program at five campuses
  4. Phase 4 (6 months): District-wide rollout with ongoing support
  5. Phase 5 (Ongoing): Continuous evaluation and refinement

Beyond the numbers, Judson ISD has seen significant qualitative improvements:

  • Earlier intervention: Students are being connected with support services before crisis situations develop
  • Improved coordination: Better communication between counselors, teachers, administrators, and community providers
  • Increased awareness: Reduced stigma around mental health issues throughout the school community
  • More equitable access: Students across all campuses now have similar access to mental health resources

"The early warning system has been a game-changer. Last year, we identified a student whose attendance and grades were dropping rapidly. Through our system, we discovered she was dealing with significant anxiety and family challenges. We were able to connect her with appropriate resources, and she's now back on track academically and socially."

Key Lessons Learned

Throughout their implementation process, Judson ISD identified several important lessons:

  • Start with clear data privacy protocols to ensure student information is protected while still allowing for effective intervention
  • Invest heavily in staff training to ensure technology tools are used appropriately and effectively
  • Build in regular evaluation cycles to identify what's working and what needs adjustment
  • Engage all stakeholders from the beginning, including parents, students, teachers, and community partners
  • Balance technology with human connection â€“ digital tools should enhance, not replace, personal relationships

Future Directions

Building on their success, Judson ISD is now expanding their mental health initiative in several directions:

  • Enhanced parent portal providing resources for supporting student mental health at home
  • Expansion of telehealth services to include specialized support for specific mental health conditions
  • Development of student peer support programs guided by trained advisors
  • Integration of wellness curriculum into regular classroom instruction

Through their comprehensive approach combining technology, staff development, and community partnerships, Judson ISD has created a sustainable system that supports student mental health and contributes to academic success.
